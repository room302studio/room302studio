# Subway Builder

Room 302 Studio supported the development and launch of Subway Builder, a groundbreaking transit simulation game created by studio member Colin Miller. The game transforms the complex challenge of urban transit planning into an engaging, data-driven experience that's captured the attention of transit enthusiasts and urban planners worldwide.

**From Broken Trains to Building Systems**

Colin's inspiration came from a quintessentially New York experience: being stuck on a broken-down 6 train during his commute. Rather than simply complaining about transit failures, he channeled that frustration into creating a tool that lets anyone explore the intricate challenges of building and operating subway systems.

**Real Data, Real Challenges**

What sets Subway Builder apart is its commitment to realism. The game leverages actual U.S. Census Bureau and employment data to simulate millions of realistic commuters across 26 major U.S. cities. Players must navigate the same complex variables that real transit planners face:

- Geographic constraints and construction challenges
- Budget limitations (starting with $3 billion in standard mode)
- Passenger behavior and route optimization
- Station overcrowding and service delays
- Infrastructure decisions between tunnels, viaducts, and cut-and-cover construction

**From Concept to Launch: The Studio's Role**

Room 302 Studio provided comprehensive support throughout Subway Builder's journey from initial concept to successful launch, embodying our 0-60 approach to bringing ambitious ideas to life.

In the early stages, the studio served as an incubator for Colin's vision. We provided critical feedback on user experience design, helping iterate on the game's interface and interaction patterns to ensure complex transit data remained accessible and engaging. Our team conducted regular design reviews, stress-tested early prototypes, and helped refine the core gameplay mechanics that would make the simulation both realistic and enjoyable.

As development accelerated, Room 302 Studio stepped in with engineering management support. We helped establish sustainable development workflows, implement version control best practices, and create a roadmap that balanced Colin's ambitious feature set with realistic launch timelines. This project management infrastructure proved crucial as the game grew in scope and complexity.

When it came time to build a community around Subway Builder, the studio played a hands-on role in establishing and moderating the Discord server. We helped structure channels for beta testing feedback, bug reports, and feature requests, creating a feedback loop between Colin and early adopters. The studio facilitated conversations with potential contributors and helped onboard additional developers who could assist with specific technical challenges, from pathfinding optimization to cross-platform compatibility.

This comprehensive support—from UX iteration to engineering management to community building—exemplifies Room 302 Studio's skunkworks approach: we don't just provide advice, we roll up our sleeves and help talented creators navigate every challenge between inspiration and impact.

**Impact and Reception**

Since its October 2024 launch, Subway Builder has captured widespread attention. [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91421609/this-addictive-game-is-like-simcity-but-for-transit-nerds) called it "addictive" and compared it to SimCity for transit nerds, while [Planetizen](https://www.planetizen.com/news/2025/10/136214-nerd-alert-subway-builder-video-game-lets-you-build-your-dream-transit-system) highlighted how players can build their dream transit systems. [NY1 featured the game](https://ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/CTV/2025/09/20/subway-builder-allows-players-to-design-their-own-transit-system) for its approach to letting players design their own transit systems, and [80.lv showcased](https://80.lv/articles/build-best-subway-system-in-this-hyperrealistic-transit-simulation-game) its hyperrealistic simulation capabilities. Even [The Banner explored](https://www.thebanner.com/community/transportation/subway-builder-baltimore-transit-system-VPYKWAWVZZGZ7BSAQ6H6J6ZFVQ/) whether the game could help solve real public transit challenges.

The game has resonated with both casual players looking for a SimCity-style experience and serious transit advocates who use it to explore real-world planning scenarios.

With support for Windows, macOS, and Linux, and available in English, Spanish, and French, Subway Builder demonstrates how Room 302 Studio helps turn bold ideas into polished products that reach global audiences.

**Technical Achievement**

Colin brought together his background in software engineering and data analysis to create sophisticated pathfinding algorithms that mirror real commuter decision-making. The game processes massive datasets to generate realistic passenger flows, showing how variables like wait times, transfers, and income distribution affect route selection—the same considerations that shape real-world transit systems.

The project exemplifies Room 302 Studio's mission: supporting innovators who transform complex challenges into elegant, engaging solutions that make a real impact.
